Hi, I am
Gustavo Padovan


I am Linux Core Technologies Lead, Client Relationship Manager and Partner at Collabora, a well respected Open Source Software consultancy that helps top companies in the world to create the next generation of technology with Open Source Software inside. Along with Sjoerd Simons we lead the Core team inside Collabora, that supports clients on hardware enablement, kernel development, base platform and generic userspace needs. I also co- manage the relationship with one the best Collabora clients. Collabora's mission is to accelerate the adoption of Open Source throughout the industry.


After many years traveling around the globe attending and speaking at Open Source and Linux conferences I felt we did not have any forum in Brazil to talk about upstream development of technologies from the Linux world.In 2017 I invited two of my best friends, Bruno Dilly and João Moreira, and together we co-founded the Linux Developer Conference Brazil - linuxdev-br. A conference born to be international and act as a meeting point for Brazilian and international community. See our mission.

I spent a decade as developer and maintainer in the Linux Kernel community. During that time I worked in the Bluetooth subsystem as developer and co-maintainer. Later I moved on the graphics and media area, where I developed for drm and media subsystem where I help with development of the Atomic Modesetting API and added suppor to Explicit Synchronization to mainline Linux Kernel helping unifying the Android and Linux generic stacks.

During high-school, in 2004, I was also doing technical formation on programming I remember one day they announced a Linux workshop to be given by a FOSS group from the University of Campinas (Unicamp) in Brazil. That group was called GPSL (Grupo Pró Software Livre in Portuguese) and they teached us very basic things in GNU/Linux like the 'ls' command, it was my fisrt exposure ever to Free Software and its filosofy, It hooked me! 2 years later I was a Computer Science freshemen at Unicamp and met GPSL again and quickly became one of the group's leader or "president" as my friends there - the same guys teached me about GNU/Linux that day at high-school. The rest is history! The cute flying cow you see here was GPSL mascot. :)
